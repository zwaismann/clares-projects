2. Emergency Notice

IF YOU OR SOMEONE ELSE IS EXPERIENCING A MEDICAL EMERGENCY OR A SUSPECTED DRUG OVERDOSE, CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY.

Do not use this website or any information on it to seek or substitute for emergency medical assistance. This website is not staffed, is not monitored in real time, and cannot provide emergency medical assistance of any kind at any time.

If you believe someone may be experiencing an opioid overdose, call 911 immediately, administer naloxone (Narcan) if available, and stay with the person until emergency services arrive.

For immediate, free, and confidential substance use crisis support:

  • SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 - Free, confidential, 24/7, English and Spanish
  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: Call or text 988 - 24/7 mental health and substance use crisis support
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 - Free, confidential, 24/7
  • Emergency Services: 911
